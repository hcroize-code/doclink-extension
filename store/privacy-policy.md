# Privacy Policy — DocLink Chrome Extension

**Effective date:** April 3, 2026
**Last updated:** April 3, 2026

---

## 1. Overview

DocLink ("the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that detects Google Workspace document links (Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, and Drive files) in your Gmail inbox and lets you organize them into custom folders.

This policy explains what data the Extension accesses, how it is used, and how it is stored.

---

## 2. Data accessed

### Gmail content (read-only)

The Extension uses the Gmail API with the `gmail.readonly` OAuth scope to:

- Retrieve a list of your recent email message IDs
- Read the body and headers (Subject, From) of individual emails to detect Google Workspace URLs

**The Extension cannot send, delete, modify, or forward any email.** Access is strictly read-only.

### Google account identity

The Extension uses Google OAuth 2.0 (`chrome.identity`) solely to obtain an access token that authorizes calls to the Gmail API on your behalf. The Extension does not access your Google account profile, contacts, or any other Google service.

---

## 3. Data storage

All data generated by the Extension is stored **exclusively on your local device** using Chrome's built-in `chrome.storage.local` API. This includes:

- Document links detected in your emails (URL, email subject, sender name)
- Custom folder names and folder assignments you create
- Scan timestamps and Gmail history IDs (used for efficient incremental scanning)

**No personal data is ever transmitted to any server operated by DocLink or any third party.** The only external network requests made by the Extension are:

| Destination | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `https://accounts.google.com` | OAuth 2.0 authentication |
| `https://gmail.googleapis.com` | Gmail API — reading emails to detect document URLs |

These are Google's own servers, governed by [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

---

## 4. Data sharing

The Extension does **not**:

- Sell, rent, or share your data with any third party
- Upload your emails or document links to any external server
- Use analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting services
- Track your browsing activity

---

## 5. Data retention and deletion

All data is stored locally on your device. You can delete it at any time by:

- Removing individual documents or folders within the Extension popup
- Uninstalling the Extension (Chrome removes all associated `chrome.storage` data automatically)

---

## 6. Permissions

| Permission | Why it is needed |
|---|---|
| `identity` | Authenticate with Google to obtain a Gmail API access token |
| `storage` | Save folders and document links locally on your device |
| `alarms` | Run periodic background scans without keeping the browser awake permanently |
| `notifications` | Show a desktop alert when a new document link is detected |
| Gmail API (read-only) | Read email content to find and extract Google Workspace URLs |

---

## 7. Children's privacy

The Extension is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.
